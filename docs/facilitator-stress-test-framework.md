# Facilitator Stress Test Framework

## Purpose

This framework evaluates facilitator performance under real-time immersive investigation conditions.

The goal is NOT perfection.

The goal is ensuring facilitators can:
- preserve immersion,
- manage escalation,
- protect uncertainty,
- coordinate multiple teams,
- and sustain emotional realism under pressure.

---

# Stress Test Conditions

Facilitators should be tested under:
- simultaneous team escalation,
- conflicting participant interpretations,
- QR interruptions,
- environmental pacing changes,
- participant emotional escalation,
- and timeline instability.

---

# Evaluation Areas

## Immersion Preservation

Verify facilitators:
- avoid classroom behavior,
- avoid lecture language,
- avoid over-explaining,
- and maintain investigative tone.

---

## Escalation Management

Verify facilitators:
- pace contradictions intentionally,
- preserve productive uncertainty,
- avoid premature clarification,
- and sustain narrative tension.

---

## Collaborative Moderation

Verify facilitators:
- encourage collaborative synthesis,
- avoid dominating discussion,
- redirect gently,
- and preserve participant ownership.

---

## Multi-Team Coordination

Verify facilitators can:
- track independent escalation states,
- balance attention effectively,
- maintain differentiated pacing,
- and avoid collapsing sessions together.

---

# Drift Detection During Stress

Watch carefully for facilitators reverting into:
- teaching,
- lecturing,
- over-instructing,
- classroom discussion leadership,
- or educational workshop behavior.

Stress often reveals hidden drift tendencies.

---

# Final Success Standard

Facilitators succeed when:
- participants remain immersed,
- uncertainty remains central,
- collaborative reasoning remains participant-driven,
- and the investigation feels emotionally authentic.
