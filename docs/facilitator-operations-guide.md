# MedDetectives Facilitator Operations Guide

## Purpose

This guide exists to help facilitators orchestrate an immersive investigative experience while preserving uncertainty, emotional realism, collaborative reasoning, and room authority.

The facilitator is not a lecturer.
The facilitator is not a classroom instructor.
The facilitator is not a content expert giving answers.

The facilitator is:
- a pacing architect,
- an escalation manager,
- a contradiction releaser,
- a narrative conductor,
- and a protector of productive ambiguity.

---

# Core Operational Philosophy

## The Room Is Primary

Participants should physically:
- move through the room,
- reconstruct timelines,
- compare evidence,
- uncover artifacts,
- debate interpretations,
- and collaboratively synthesize meaning.

The digital system supports the room.
The digital system should never dominate the room.

---

# Facilitator Priorities

In order of importance:

1. Preserve immersion
2. Preserve uncertainty
3. Preserve collaborative reasoning
4. Pace escalation intentionally
5. Avoid rescuing teams too quickly
6. Protect emotional safety
7. Guide reflection without over-directing

---

# Pre-Session Setup

## Room Preparation

Verify:
- Evidence packets placed correctly
- QR markers functional and visible
- Timeline cards mixed intentionally
- Hidden artifacts secured
- Escalation envelopes staged
- Audio system operational
- Environmental lighting operational
- Facilitator dashboard open
- Session timer ready

---

# Session Activation

## Before Participants Enter

Facilitator should:
- activate the session,
- verify timer readiness,
- confirm room-state initialization,
- confirm escalation assets are accessible,
- and review planned contradiction release pacing.

Avoid over-explaining the experience.
Mystery and uncertainty should begin immediately.

---

# Escalation Philosophy

## Productive Uncertainty

Participants SHOULD:
- feel uncertain,
- revise assumptions,
- disagree collaboratively,
- experience interpretation instability,
- and revisit earlier conclusions.

This is not failure.
This is the experience.

---

# When To Intervene

Intervene when:
- teams become completely stalled,
- conflict becomes personally unproductive,
- emotional overwhelm emerges,
- escalation pacing collapses,
- or interpretation certainty becomes rigid too early.

Do NOT intervene simply because participants are struggling.

---

# Contradiction Release Strategy

Contradictions should:
- destabilize certainty,
- complicate interpretation,
- deepen emotional complexity,
- and redirect investigation pathways.

Contradictions should NOT:
- provide answers,
- clarify the entire scenario,
- or collapse ambiguity.

---

# Environmental Modulation

Facilitators may adjust:
- lighting intensity,
- audio texture,
- pacing speed,
- silence duration,
- evidence release timing,
- and room focus.

Environmental changes should support emotional and interpretive escalation.

---

# Debrief Philosophy

The debrief is NOT:
- a quiz review,
- a correctness evaluation,
- or a teaching lecture.

The debrief IS:
- collaborative reflection,
- reasoning reconstruction,
- assumption analysis,
- uncertainty examination,
- and emotional processing.

Participants should defend reasoning journeys rather than final answers.

---

# Reset Procedures

After each session:
- restore evidence placements,
- reset QR states,
- reseal escalation packets,
- clear collaborative boards,
- reset timer state,
- verify hidden artifacts,
- and restore environmental defaults.

---

# Final Facilitator Reminder

Do not over-facilitate.

The moment the experience feels like:
- school,
- a workshop,
- a lecture,
- or an educational activity,

…the illusion collapses.

Your role is to sustain investigation, uncertainty, emotional realism, and collaborative meaning-making.
