Jordan Carter Case — Facilitator Operations Packet

Facilitator-ready immersive investigation materials designed to support collaborative reasoning, uncertainty navigation, emotional realism, and room-first investigative synthesis.

1. Room Setup Guide

The Jordan Carter Case should feel like a living investigation space rather than a classroom or game station. Lighting should be softened when possible. Investigation materials should be distributed intentionally throughout the room to encourage movement, collaborative interpretation, emotional discovery, and evolving uncertainty.

  • Zone A — Initial Evidence Table: witness statements, folders, timeline fragments, observation cards, participant journals
  • Zone B — Integrated QR Discovery Area: hidden QR sheets beneath evidence, behind clipboards, inside folders, and embedded naturally within investigative materials
  • Zone C — Collaborative Evidence Board: physical timeline reconstruction, evolving interpretations, evidence connections, collaborative synthesis
  • Zone D — Restricted Observation Station: sealed escalation envelopes, facilitator-controlled contradiction releases, hidden access PIN artifacts
  • Facilitator station visible but intentionally non-dominant
  • Collaborative seating arranged to encourage discussion, movement, and interpretive disagreement

2. Setup Checklist

  • Print all evidence artifacts
  • Print and stage QR assets naturally throughout the room
  • Arrange physical timeline reconstruction materials
  • Prepare facilitator contradiction envelopes
  • Prepare escalation evidence envelopes
  • Set up collaborative evidence board
  • Prepare participant journals and torn fragments
  • Place contradictory observation evidence separately
  • Verify facilitator dashboard access
  • Verify participant activation device
  • Verify shared session timer synchronization between facilitator and participant views
  • Place markers, clips, evidence tags, and collaborative materials throughout the room
  • Verify environmental audio and lighting readiness

12. Facilitator Pacing Guide

  • 0–10 Minutes — Initial exploration, environmental orientation, and early collaborative interpretation
  • 10–25 Minutes — Conflicting narrative tension, QR discoveries, and emotional-context interpretation shifts
  • 25–40 Minutes — Physical timeline reconstruction and collaborative synthesis instability
  • 40–55 Minutes — Contradictory evidence escalation, reinterpretation, and emotional destabilization
  • 55–60 Minutes — Reflective synthesis, unresolved tension discussion, and debrief transition
