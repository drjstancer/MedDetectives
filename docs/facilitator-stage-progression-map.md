# Facilitator Stage Progression Map

## Purpose

This guide helps facilitators determine when to advance investigation stages during the Jordan Carter experience.

Stages should advance based on:
- collaborative investigation momentum,
- reasoning instability,
- interpretive evolution,
- and emotional escalation.

Stages should NOT advance solely because of time.

The investigation should feel alive rather than mechanically sequenced.

---

# Core Philosophy

Facilitators are not moving participants through:
- classroom checkpoints,
- lesson modules,
- or game levels.

Facilitators are modulating:
- uncertainty,
- collaborative reasoning,
- emotional tension,
- and interpretive destabilization.

Key discoveries should feel:
- naturally uncovered,
- emotionally meaningful,
- collaboratively interpreted,
- and strategically destabilizing.

The goal is NOT revealing everything.

The goal is controlling:
- interpretive pressure,
- collaborative uncertainty,
- and emotional escalation.

---

# STAGE 1 — Orientation & Initial Investigation

## Purpose

Establish:
- curiosity,
- room exploration,
- initial evidence synthesis,
- and collaborative engagement.

---

## Expected Participant Behaviors

Participants should:
- explore physically,
- begin debating evidence,
- organize materials,
- form early assumptions,
- and identify initial tensions.

---

## Recommended Discoveries During This Stage

Participants should primarily discover:
- initial witness packets,
- basic timeline fragments,
- introductory observation cards,
- visible evidence board materials,
- and environmental clues.

QR discoveries during this stage should:
- reinforce curiosity,
- deepen exploration,
- and create subtle tension.

Avoid major destabilization too early.

Participants should still feel relatively confident in their early interpretations.

---

## Facilitator Focus

Protect:
- immersion,
- movement,
- collaboration,
- and uncertainty.

Avoid:
- over-explaining,
- over-guiding,
- or clarifying contradictions too early.

---

## Advance Stage When:

- most teams have begun forming interpretations,
- collaborative discussion becomes active,
- evidence clustering begins naturally,
- and participants appear increasingly confident in early assumptions.

This confidence is important because later stages destabilize it.

---

# STAGE 2 — Emerging Contradictions

## Purpose

Introduce:
- conflicting interpretations,
- emotional instability,
- timeline tension,
- and uncertainty escalation.

---

## Expected Participant Behaviors

Participants should:
- disagree more actively,
- revisit evidence,
- question earlier assumptions,
- and begin collaborative renegotiation.

---

## Recommended Discoveries During This Stage

Participants should begin discovering:
- emotionally conflicting witness statements,
- hidden journal fragments,
- QR reveals tied to emotional-context evidence,
- and subtle timeline inconsistencies.

Suggested reveals:
- QR-01 Timeline Tension Discovery
- early emotional masking journal fragments
- contradictory observational details

PIN systems should begin emerging during this stage but should NOT yet dominate participant focus.

PIN discoveries should feel like:
- investigative validation,
- not puzzle progression.

---

## Facilitator Focus

Encourage:
- reinterpretation,
- evidence comparison,
- and uncertainty tolerance.

Avoid:
- resolving contradictions,
- stabilizing interpretations too quickly,
- or steering participants toward “correct” answers.

---

## Advance Stage When:

- teams begin revising earlier assumptions,
- timeline instability becomes visible,
- collaborative disagreement intensifies,
- and emotional-context evidence becomes more central to interpretation.

---

# STAGE 3 — Timeline Reconstruction Instability

## Purpose

Push participants toward:
- collaborative synthesis,
- evidence restructuring,
- and interpretation destabilization.

---

## Expected Participant Behaviors

Participants should:
- physically reorganize timeline elements,
- challenge evidence reliability,
- debate memory accuracy,
- and negotiate competing interpretations.

---

## Recommended Discoveries During This Stage

This is the primary stage for:
- timeline reconstruction instability,
- major QR reveals,
- and collaborative reinterpretation.

Suggested reveals:
- QR-02 Witness Perspective Shift
- Access PIN 4821
- contradictory timeline evidence
- observation inconsistencies
- hidden witness reinterpretations

Journal fragments should begin reshaping emotional interpretation significantly.

Participants should begin recognizing:
- confidence and accuracy are not always aligned.

---

## Facilitator Focus

Protect:
- productive instability,
- collaborative ownership,
- and emotional realism.

Avoid:
- confirming interpretations,
- correcting reasoning prematurely,
- or reducing ambiguity.

---

## Advance Stage When:

- most groups demonstrate visible interpretive instability,
- collaborative synthesis becomes emotionally engaged,
- contradictions are actively influencing reasoning,
- and teams appear uncertain in meaningful ways.

---

# STAGE 4 — Emotional Escalation & Destabilization

## Purpose

Intensify:
- emotional realism,
- uncertainty,
- collaborative tension,
- and reflective discomfort.

---

## Expected Participant Behaviors

Participants should:
- revisit earlier judgments,
- question emotional assumptions,
- engage deeply in collaborative reflection,
- and wrestle with unresolved uncertainty.

---

## Recommended Discoveries During This Stage

This stage should contain:
- the strongest destabilization reveals,
- facilitator-controlled escalation discoveries,
- and emotionally significant reinterpretations.

Suggested reveals:
- QR-03 Escalation Observation Discovery
- Access PIN 7314
- sealed contradiction envelopes
- late-stage emotional-context evidence
- facilitator-controlled reflective prompts

Participants should feel:
- emotionally unsettled,
- interpretively unstable,
- and collaboratively reflective.

This stage is NOT about solving the investigation.

It is about destabilizing certainty.

---

## Facilitator Focus

Sustain:
- emotional grounding,
- reflective instability,
- and collaborative meaning-making.

Avoid:
- collapsing ambiguity,
- rushing toward answers,
- or over-facilitating.

---

## Advance Stage When:

- participants shift from answer-seeking toward reflective reasoning,
- emotional complexity becomes central,
- and teams demonstrate evolved understanding rather than certainty.

---

# STAGE 5 — Reflective Synthesis & Debrief Transition

## Purpose

Transition participants toward:
- reasoning reflection,
- collaborative synthesis,
- emotional processing,
- and uncertainty analysis.

---

## Expected Participant Behaviors

Participants should:
- discuss evolving interpretations,
- identify assumption changes,
- reflect collaboratively,
- and analyze reasoning journeys.

---

## Recommended Discoveries During This Stage

No major evidence should emerge during this stage.

Instead participants should:
- revisit prior discoveries,
- reinterpret earlier evidence,
- and synthesize collaborative meaning.

This stage should feel:
- reflective,
- emotionally grounded,
- unresolved in meaningful ways,
- and intellectually human.

---

## Facilitator Focus

Encourage:
- reflection,
- synthesis,
- emotional processing,
- and interpretive complexity.

Avoid:
- simplistic closure,
- “correct answer” framing,
- or lecture-style debriefing.

---

# Final Facilitator Reminder

The goal is NOT progressing through stages efficiently.

The goal is sustaining:
- uncertainty,
- collaboration,
- emotional realism,
- and evolving interpretation.

The experience should feel:
- human,
- unstable,
- reflective,
- and alive.
