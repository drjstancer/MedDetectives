# Error Recovery Guide

## Purpose

This guide helps facilitators recover from technical or operational interruptions without collapsing immersion or disrupting collaborative investigation.

Participants should continue experiencing:
- uncertainty,
- investigation,
- collaboration,
- and narrative tension

even during recovery moments.

---

# Recovery Philosophy

Do not panic.

Facilitators should preserve:
- emotional continuity,
- narrative realism,
- and collaborative engagement.

The room should remain authoritative even if digital systems fail temporarily.

---

# QR Failure Recovery

If QR systems fail:

- provide physical backup evidence,
- verbally frame the transition naturally,
- maintain investigative pacing,
- and avoid technical explanations dominating the room.

Participants should not feel removed from the investigation.

---

# Timer Failure Recovery

If timer systems fail:

- continue facilitator pacing manually,
- maintain escalation timing approximately,
- preserve urgency through facilitation tone,
- and continue environmental progression.

---

# Environmental Failure Recovery

If lighting or audio systems fail:

- continue narrative pacing,
- preserve uncertainty verbally,
- maintain contradiction timing,
- and avoid stopping the investigation unless absolutely necessary.

---

# Participant Confusion Recovery

If teams become severely disoriented:

- redirect gently,
- reinforce unresolved tensions,
- encourage collaborative reassessment,
- and avoid solving the investigation directly.

---

# Facilitator Reminder

Operational recovery should feel:
- adaptive,
- immersive,
- and narratively grounded.

Never let technical disruption transform the experience into:
- classroom troubleshooting,
- software debugging,
- or instructional interruption.
