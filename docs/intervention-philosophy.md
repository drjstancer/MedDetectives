# Intervention Philosophy

## Core Principle

Facilitator interventions exist to:
- preserve collaborative momentum,
- deepen synthesis,
- maintain emotional realism,
- and sustain interpretive movement.

Interventions should support the investigation without dominating it.

---

# Human-Centered Facilitation

Facilitators remain:
- conductors,
- moderators,
- and collaborative reasoning guides.

Recommendations should support:
- pacing,
- contradiction awareness,
- reinterpretation,
- and synthesis.

The facilitator determines:
- whether intervention occurs,
- when escalation is appropriate,
- and how ambiguity is preserved.

---

# Appropriate Interventions

Appropriate interventions include:
- redirecting attention toward overlooked evidence,
- encouraging collaborative synthesis,
- releasing contradictions strategically,
- or prompting reinterpretation.

Interventions should avoid:
- providing direct answers,
- collapsing ambiguity,
- or steering participants toward singular conclusions.

---

# Anti-Drift Guardrail

Intervention systems should never become:
- gameplay hint engines,
- deterministic guidance systems,
- participant correction systems,
- or algorithmic facilitation replacements.

The investigation remains:
- collaborative,
- interpretive,
- uncertain,
- and human-centered.

---

# Vision Alignment

The room remains the immersive experience.

The technology layer exists only to support:
- facilitator awareness,
- pacing continuity,
- synchronization,
- and collaborative investigation.
