# MedDetectives

## Vision

MedDetectives is an immersive, facilitator-led educational investigation experience for undergraduate pre-med learners.

The physical room is the authoritative experience.

Participants physically:
- reconstruct timelines,
- synthesize evidence,
- compare conflicting witness interpretations,
- uncover hidden artifacts,
- challenge assumptions,
- and collaboratively interpret escalating evidence.

The digital layer exists to:
- pace escalation,
- validate discoveries,
- release contradictions,
- synchronize progression,
- unlock investigative reveals,
- support facilitator orchestration,
- and intensify collaborative reasoning.

The experience should feel like:
- a live investigative command center,
- an immersive academic thriller,
- a collaborative evidence synthesis environment,
- and a high-tension reasoning experience.

It should never feel like:
- online coursework,
- educational dashboards,
- quiz software,
- or traditional edtech.

---

## Core Design Philosophy

### The Room Is Primary

The room itself contains:
- evidence packets,
- witness statements,
- hidden artifacts,
- QR markers,
- physical timelines,
- collaborative synthesis boards,
- escalation envelopes,
- and facilitator-triggered contradiction releases.

The app supports the room.
The app never replaces the room.

---

## Clinical Reasoning Through Gameplay

Clinical reasoning is embedded through the investigation experience.

Participants develop reasoning by:
- navigating uncertainty,
- confronting conflicting evidence,
- revising assumptions,
- weighing incomplete information,
- recognizing ambiguity,
- and collaboratively defending interpretations.

Confidence does not equal accuracy.

---

## Facilitator Philosophy

Facilitators are:
- investigation conductors,
- escalation managers,
- pacing architects,
- contradiction releasers,
- and collaborative reasoning moderators.

Facilitators strategically:
- release destabilizing evidence,
- inject contradictions,
- escalate tension,
- modulate environmental pacing,
- and preserve productive ambiguity.

Facilitators should not provide direct answers or rescue participants from uncertainty.

---

## Experience Goals

The goal is not to produce a single correct answer.

The goal is to develop:
- collaborative reasoning,
- evidence interpretation,
- uncertainty management,
- reflective synthesis,
- emotional intelligence,
- and human-centered clinical thinking.
