# Mobile Room Usage Verification

## Purpose

This verification process ensures mobile devices support the physical investigation experience without replacing it.

Phones should function as:
- investigative tools,
- evidence scanners,
- escalation reveal devices,
- and collaborative support systems.

Phones should NOT become the primary focus of the experience.

---

# Verification Principles

Participants should spend more time:
- moving,
- discussing,
- debating,
- synthesizing,
- and physically interacting with evidence

than staring at screens.

If the experience becomes screen-dominant, recalibration is required.

---

# QR Interaction Verification

Verify:
- QR scan speed,
- low-light scanning reliability,
- movement-based scanning usability,
- escalation reveal loading speed,
- and evidence readability after scanning.

---

# Physical Movement Verification

Verify:
- participants naturally move throughout the room,
- collaborative clusters form organically,
- phones do not isolate participants,
- and physical evidence remains central.

---

# Interface Verification

Verify:
- buttons remain readable while standing,
- evidence displays remain concise,
- stage progression visibility remains clear,
- and environmental pacing does not require excessive screen interaction.

---

# Facilitator Verification

Verify:
- facilitators can operate while moving,
- escalation triggers remain accessible,
- intervention guidance remains readable quickly,
- and facilitator attention remains focused on participants rather than screens.

---

# Final Mobile Philosophy

Mobile devices should disappear into the investigation.

Participants should remember:
- the uncertainty,
- the evidence,
- the collaboration,
- the contradictions,
- and the emotional realism.

Not the interface.
