# Modular Facilitation Language Library

## Purpose

This language library helps facilitators preserve immersion while guiding collaborative reasoning, uncertainty navigation, and investigative escalation.

These are NOT scripts.

These are modular intervention prompts intended to:
- redirect,
- destabilize,
- deepen interpretation,
- encourage reflection,
- and sustain uncertainty.

---

# Re-Engagement Prompts

- “Something about the sequence still feels unstable.”
- “Your confidence may be outpacing the evidence.”
- “There may be a connection your team hasn’t fully explored yet.”
- “The room still contains unresolved tension.”
- “Not every clue carries equal weight.”

---

# Contradiction Escalation Prompts

- “Emotionally honest accounts are not always factually reliable.”
- “A confident interpretation can still be incomplete.”
- “The evidence may support more than one interpretation right now.”
- “What assumption would collapse if this evidence were true?”
- “You may be stabilizing the story too early.”

---

# Collaborative Reasoning Prompts

- “Where does your team disagree right now?”
- “Which interpretation currently feels weakest?”
- “What evidence are you trusting most heavily?”
- “Has the investigation changed your understanding of Jordan?”
- “What remains unresolved?”

---

# Emotional Reflection Prompts

- “What aspects of this case feel increasingly human rather than clinical?”
- “What emotional signals may have been overlooked initially?”
- “How has the emotional context shifted your interpretation?”
- “What assumptions did your team make early that no longer feel stable?”

---

# Stalled Team Prompts

- “You may need to revisit an earlier interpretation.”
- “Some evidence becomes more important later in the investigation.”
- “What evidence has your team discounted too quickly?”
- “There may still be unresolved contradictions in the room.”

---

# Debrief Transition Prompts

- “Defend your reasoning journey, not just your conclusions.”
- “What changed most dramatically during the investigation?”
- “Where did uncertainty become productive?”
- “What assumptions became unstable over time?”
- “What contradictions most altered your interpretation?”

---

# Facilitator Reminder

Avoid sounding like:
- a lecturer,
- a classroom facilitator,
- or a workshop instructor.

Facilitators should sound:
- observational,
- investigative,
- reflective,
- emotionally grounded,
- and intentionally uncertain.

Preserve the investigation.
Do not solve it for participants.
