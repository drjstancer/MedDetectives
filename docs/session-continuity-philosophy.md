# Session Continuity Philosophy

## Core Principle

Participants should experience:
- sustained immersion,
- collaborative continuity,
- emotional realism,
- and uninterrupted investigation pacing.

The infrastructure exists to preserve the human experience.

---

# Continuity Priorities

The system prioritizes:
- facilitator continuity,
- participant recovery,
- synchronized escalation,
- and collaborative momentum.

Technical systems should remain operationally invisible whenever possible.

---

# Session Recovery Philosophy

Recovery systems should:
- quietly restore state,
- preserve pacing,
- protect collaborative synthesis,
- and avoid breaking immersion.

Participants should not feel like they are recovering software.

Participants should feel like the investigation is continuing.

---

# Archival Philosophy

Archived sessions are not grades.

They are reflective continuity records intended to support:
- facilitator growth,
- future refinement,
- and operational learning.

The experience remains human-centered.

---

# Vision Alignment

The room remains the immersive authority.

The technology layer exists to:
- support pacing,
- support escalation,
- support continuity,
- and support facilitator orchestration.
