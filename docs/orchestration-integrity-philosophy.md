# Orchestration Integrity Philosophy

## Core Principle

Operational integrity exists to preserve:
- collaborative continuity,
- emotional realism,
- facilitator pacing,
- and immersive investigation flow.

Integrity systems should remain operationally invisible whenever possible.

---

# Integrity Is Not Control

Integrity validation should never become:
- participant surveillance,
- rigid workflow enforcement,
- deterministic gameplay sequencing,
- or facilitator replacement.

Integrity systems exist only to:
- identify synchronization risk,
- preserve continuity,
- and support facilitator judgment.

---

# Human-Centered Orchestration

Facilitators remain:
- conductors,
- moderators,
- and guardians of collaborative interpretation.

Technology may:
- identify instability,
- recommend pacing review,
- or highlight continuity concerns.

The facilitator determines:
- whether escalation should continue,
- whether intervention is needed,
- and how ambiguity should be preserved.

---

# Escalation Philosophy

Escalation readiness should remain:
- interpretive,
- facilitator-guided,
- and emotionally aware.

The platform should never automatically escalate the investigation.

Human facilitation remains authoritative.

---

# Vision Alignment

The room remains the immersive authority.

The technology layer exists only to support:
- continuity,
- pacing,
- orchestration,
- and collaborative investigation.
