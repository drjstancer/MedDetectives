# QR Print Asset Package Guide

## Purpose

This guide standardizes the printing, placement, and deployment of QR investigation assets throughout the MedDetectives experience.

QR assets should feel naturally integrated into the investigation environment.

They should NOT feel:
- classroom-like,
- gamified in a cartoonish way,
- or digitally dominant.

---

# QR Placement Philosophy

QR markers should:
- support discovery,
- reinforce immersion,
- encourage movement,
- and intensify collaborative investigation.

QR markers should never become the primary experience.

The room remains authoritative.

---

# Recommended QR Asset Types

## Evidence Labels
Used for:
- witness folders,
- timeline artifacts,
- medical notes,
- communication records,
- environmental evidence,
- escalation reveals.

---

## Hidden Investigation Triggers
Used for:
- contradiction releases,
- reinterpretation moments,
- environmental escalation,
- and emotional-context discoveries.

These should be physically concealed or contextually integrated.

---

# Printing Recommendations

## Size
Recommended:
- 2in x 2in minimum
- 3in x 3in preferred for environmental scanning reliability

---

## Material
Recommended:
- matte cardstock,
- laminated evidence labels,
- weather-resistant adhesive for environmental placement,
- or textured evidence tags.

Avoid glossy surfaces that reduce scan reliability.

---

# Placement Recommendations

QR assets should:
- appear physically believable,
- blend naturally into investigative materials,
- avoid obvious clustering,
- and support movement throughout the room.

Examples:
- attached to witness folders,
- hidden inside reflection journals,
- embedded in timeline packets,
- placed beneath clipped documents,
- attached to environmental props.

---

# Escalation QR Strategy

Escalation QR reveals should:
- destabilize certainty,
- deepen ambiguity,
- alter interpretation,
- and intensify collaborative synthesis.

Escalation QR reveals should NOT:
- solve the investigation,
- provide direct answers,
- or collapse uncertainty prematurely.

---

# QR Verification Checklist

Before each session:

- [ ] All QR codes scan successfully
- [ ] QR placement matches intended evidence pathway
- [ ] Escalation QR timing verified
- [ ] Hidden QR assets remain concealed appropriately
- [ ] Mobile scanning tested under room lighting
- [ ] Physical wear inspected
- [ ] Duplicate backup QR prints available

---

# Final Reminder

QR codes are support structures.

Participants should remember:
- the investigation,
- the uncertainty,
- the collaboration,
- and the emotional realism.

Not the technology.
