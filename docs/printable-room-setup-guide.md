# Printable Room Setup Guide

## Room Philosophy

The room itself is the primary investigative experience.

Participants should physically:
- discover,
- move,
- compare,
- rearrange,
- debate,
- and synthesize evidence.

The room should feel immersive, uncertain, collaborative, and emotionally grounded.

---

# Setup Checklist

## Environmental Preparation

- [ ] Lighting configured for orientation stage
- [ ] Audio system operational
- [ ] Environmental transitions prepared
- [ ] Investigation atmosphere clear of classroom cues

---

# Evidence Placement

## Timeline Materials

- [ ] Timeline cards intentionally mixed
- [ ] Timeline reconstruction area accessible
- [ ] Contradictory timestamps distributed intentionally

## Witness Materials

- [ ] Witness packets distributed across room
- [ ] Emotional-context evidence separated strategically
- [ ] Contradictions not immediately obvious

## QR Placement

- [ ] QR markers functional
- [ ] QR markers physically integrated into environment
- [ ] Escalation QR reveals staged appropriately

## Hidden Artifacts

- [ ] Reflection materials hidden intentionally
- [ ] Escalation envelopes sealed
- [ ] Environmental clues positioned naturally

---

# Facilitator Station

Prepare:
- facilitator dashboard,
- escalation controls,
- contradiction triggers,
- environmental pacing controls,
- audio guidance,
- and debrief export access.

---

# Pre-Session Verification

Before participants enter:

- [ ] Session activation verified
- [ ] Timer reset
- [ ] QR scan flow tested
- [ ] Escalation sequence reviewed
- [ ] Contradiction release pacing prepared
- [ ] Environmental defaults restored

---

# Reset Checklist

After each session:

- [ ] Restore evidence placements
- [ ] Reset QR states
- [ ] Clear collaborative synthesis areas
- [ ] Restore hidden artifacts
- [ ] Reset escalation envelopes
- [ ] Restore lighting defaults
- [ ] Reset audio environment
- [ ] Verify facilitator dashboard state
