# Facilitator Failover Protocol

## Purpose

The facilitator failover protocol exists to preserve:
- investigation continuity,
- collaborative pacing,
- emotional realism,
- and room stability.

The protocol should remain operationally invisible to participants whenever possible.

---

# Core Principle

The investigation should never feel like:
- software crashed,
- systems failed,
- or orchestration broke.

The room remains primary.

The facilitator preserves continuity.

---

# Recommended Facilitator Responses

## Device Failure

If facilitator systems disconnect:
- continue operating from physical evidence,
- continue participant synthesis,
- continue discussion-based progression,
- and delay digital escalation reveals temporarily.

The room can continue functioning.

---

## Session Recovery

When systems reconnect:
- restore session state,
- restore stage progression,
- restore participant continuity,
- and continue escalation pacing naturally.

Avoid explicitly announcing technical recovery.

---

# Facilitator Override Conditions

Overrides may be used when:
- participants become stuck,
- pacing collapses,
- evidence is physically compromised,
- technology fails,
- or emotional escalation becomes unproductive.

Overrides should support:
- investigation integrity,
- not facilitator control.

---

# Vision Alignment

The platform supports the room.

The room remains the immersive experience.
