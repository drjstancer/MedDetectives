# MedDetectives Master Framework
## Jordan Carter Investigation Experience

---

# Core Vision

MedDetectives is an immersive collaborative investigation experience designed to develop clinical reasoning, interpretive flexibility, collaborative synthesis, uncertainty navigation, and emotional realism through a room-first investigative structure.

This experience is NOT:
- an LMS,
- a digital worksheet,
- a lecture simulation,
- a puzzle-only escape room,
- or educational software with game aesthetics.

This experience IS:
- a living investigation,
- an emotionally grounded collaborative mystery,
- a room-first reasoning environment,
- and a narrative-driven uncertainty experience.

The room remains authoritative.
The digital layer exists to support escalation, pacing, synthesis, and immersive progression.

---

# Foundational Design Principles

## 1. Room Authority

Participants should physically:
- investigate,
- move,
- collaborate,
- debate,
- reconstruct timelines,
- compare evidence,
- and synthesize interpretations.

Phones and digital systems support the room.
They should never dominate the experience.

---

## 2. Productive Uncertainty

Participants should:
- revise assumptions,
- encounter contradictions,
- destabilize interpretations,
- and tolerate unresolved ambiguity.

The experience intentionally avoids simplistic certainty.

---

## 3. Collaborative Reasoning

The experience is designed around:
- collective interpretation,
- disagreement,
- negotiation of meaning,
- and evolving synthesis.

Reasoning should emerge naturally from investigation.

---

## 4. Emotional Realism

The investigation should feel emotionally human rather than clinically detached.

Participants should experience:
- emotional complexity,
- interpretive instability,
- uncertainty tension,
- and reflective discomfort.

---

# Investigation Architecture

## Physical Experience Layer

Core room elements include:
- timeline reconstruction,
- witness packets,
- hidden artifacts,
- escalation envelopes,
- environmental clues,
- collaborative synthesis spaces,
- and immersive atmospheric design.

The timeline reconstruction process remains physical and collaborative.

---

## Digital Support Layer

Digital systems support:
- QR evidence reveals,
- escalation pacing,
- facilitator orchestration,
- shared session timing,
- contradiction progression,
- environmental guidance,
- and debrief synthesis.

The digital layer should feel ambient and supportive rather than dominant.

---

# Escalation Philosophy

Escalation should:
- destabilize certainty,
- deepen emotional complexity,
- intensify collaborative interpretation,
- and force assumption revision.

Escalation should NOT:
- provide answers,
- collapse ambiguity,
- or reduce the investigation to puzzle progression.

---

# Contradiction Design

Contradictions are central to the experience.

Contradictions should:
- complicate interpretation,
- reveal emotional nuance,
- destabilize overconfidence,
- and reinforce ambiguity.

Participants should repeatedly reconsider earlier conclusions.

---

# Facilitator Philosophy

Facilitators are NOT:
- lecturers,
- workshop leaders,
- teachers running stations,
- or content explainers.

Facilitators ARE:
- narrative conductors,
- escalation managers,
- collaborative moderators,
- and protectors of productive ambiguity.

Facilitators guide pacing while preserving participant ownership.

---

# Facilitator Operations

Facilitators are responsible for:
- session activation,
- escalation pacing,
- contradiction release timing,
- environmental modulation,
- collaborative tension management,
- emotional safety,
- and immersive continuity.

Facilitators should intervene minimally and intentionally.

---

# Modular Facilitation Language

Facilitator prompts should:
- redirect gently,
- destabilize certainty,
- preserve immersion,
- and deepen collaborative reflection.

Facilitators should avoid:
- classroom language,
- lecture framing,
- excessive explanation,
- and answer-oriented guidance.

---

# QR Philosophy

QR systems should:
- support discovery,
- encourage movement,
- reinforce immersion,
- and deepen investigation.

QR systems should NOT:
- become scavenger hunts,
- dominate participant attention,
- or replace collaborative synthesis.

---

# PIN Progression Philosophy

PIN progression should emerge from:
- evidence synthesis,
- collaborative reasoning,
- and interpretive discovery.

PIN systems should not feel arbitrary or excessively game-like.

---

# Environmental Modulation

Environmental systems include:
- lighting shifts,
- audio escalation,
- room-state transitions,
- and pacing modulation.

Environmental changes should reinforce emotional and interpretive escalation.

---

# Multi-Team Orchestration

Multiple teams should:
- maintain differentiated experiences,
- experience independent escalation,
- and preserve collaborative uniqueness.

The experience should never feel standardized or classroom synchronized.

---

# Debrief Philosophy

The debrief is NOT:
- a quiz review,
- a correctness evaluation,
- or a lecture.

The debrief IS:
- reflective reconstruction,
- reasoning analysis,
- emotional processing,
- and collaborative synthesis.

Participants defend reasoning journeys rather than final answers.

---

# Drift Protection Principles

The project must continuously guard against drift into:
- LMS behavior,
- worksheet mechanics,
- over-gamification,
- lecture facilitation,
- puzzle-only engagement,
- or screen-dominant interaction.

The experience succeeds when participants remember:
- the investigation,
- the uncertainty,
- the contradictions,
- the emotional realism,
- and the collaborative reasoning.

Not the technology.

# Final Experience Standard

MedDetectives succeeds when participants feel:
- intellectually challenged,
- emotionally invested,
- collaboratively engaged,
- interpretively destabilized,
- and reflectively transformed.

The experience should feel like:
- a living investigation,
- an unfolding emotional mystery,
- and a collaborative reasoning journey.

- school with props.
