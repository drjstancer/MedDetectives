Jordan Carter Case — Facilitator Operations Packet

Facilitator-ready hybrid educational investigation materials designed to support collaborative reasoning, immersive learning, facilitator-guided synthesis, and cinematic educational discovery.

1. Room Setup Guide

The Jordan Carter Case should feel like a collaborative educational investigation space rather than a traditional classroom. Lighting should be softened when possible. Investigation materials should be spread intentionally across the room to encourage movement, collaborative synthesis, and iterative reasoning.

  • Zone A — Initial Evidence Table: witness statements, folders, participant journals, sticky notes, timeline fragments
  • Zone B — QR Discovery Area: hidden QR sheets under tables, behind clipboards, inside folders, near evidence props
  • Zone C — Collaborative Evidence Board: timelines, string connections, evolving interpretations, sticky notes, dry erase reasoning
  • Zone D — Restricted Observation Station: sealed envelopes, escalation evidence, facilitator-controlled discoveries, access PIN artifacts
  • Facilitator station visible but non-dominant
  • Collaborative seating arranged for group synthesis and discussion

2. Setup Checklist

  • Print all evidence artifacts
  • Print QR sheets and hide throughout room
  • Arrange timeline reconstruction materials
  • Prepare facilitator clue envelopes
  • Prepare escalation discovery envelopes
  • Set up collaborative evidence board
  • Prepare participant journals and torn fragments
  • Place contradictory observation evidence separately
  • Verify facilitator dashboard access
  • Verify participant activation device
  • Verify countdown synchronization between facilitator and participant views
  • Place sticky notes, markers, clips, and evidence tags around room

12. Facilitator Pacing Guide

  • 0–10 Minutes — Initial evidence exploration and environmental orientation
  • 10–25 Minutes — Conflicting narrative tension and QR discoveries
  • 25–40 Minutes — Timeline reconstruction and collaborative synthesis
  • 40–55 Minutes — Contradictory evidence escalation and reinterpretation
  • 55–60 Minutes — Final synthesis, reflection, and debrief preparation

13. Debrief Prompts

  • Which evidence most influenced your early assumptions?
  • What caused your group to reconsider its interpretation?
  • How did your group navigate disagreement?
  • Which clue shifted your reasoning most significantly?
  • How does uncertainty influence collaborative reasoning?
  • How does this experience mirror real-world clinical reasoning challenges?
