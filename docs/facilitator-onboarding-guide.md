# Facilitator Onboarding Guide

## Purpose

This onboarding guide prepares facilitators to conduct MedDetectives experiences while preserving immersion, uncertainty, collaborative reasoning, and emotional realism.

Facilitators are not instructors delivering content.

Facilitators are:
- narrative conductors,
- escalation managers,
- collaborative moderators,
- and protectors of productive ambiguity.

---

# Facilitator Mindset

Participants should:
- investigate physically,
- interpret collaboratively,
- disagree productively,
- revise assumptions,
- and experience evolving uncertainty.

The facilitator should not:
- provide answers,
- lecture participants,
- over-explain evidence,
- or eliminate ambiguity.

---

# Before Your First Session

Review:
- Facilitator Operations Guide
- Modular Facilitation Language Library
- Jordan Carter scenario flow
- Evidence packet structure
- Escalation pacing logic
- Contradiction release timing

Practice:
- QR scanning flow
- Session activation
- Escalation triggers
- Environmental pacing controls
- Debrief transition flow

---

# During The Session

Monitor:
- team confidence levels,
- stalled reasoning,
- collaborative disagreement,
- emotional escalation,
- interpretation rigidity,
- and unresolved contradictions.

Avoid excessive intervention.

Productive uncertainty is part of the experience.

---

# Common Facilitator Errors

Do NOT:
- solve the case for teams,
- turn the experience into teaching,
- over-direct investigation,
- explain clues prematurely,
- rush uncertainty resolution,
- or dominate collaborative synthesis.

---

# Successful Facilitation Looks Like

Successful sessions include:
- evolving interpretations,
- assumption revision,
- collaborative debate,
- emotional investment,
- reflective synthesis,
- and productive ambiguity.

Teams should leave feeling:
- intellectually challenged,
- emotionally reflective,
- collaboratively invested,
- and uncertain in meaningful ways.
