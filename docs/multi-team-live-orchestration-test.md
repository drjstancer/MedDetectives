# Multi-Team Live Orchestration Test

## Purpose

This framework validates whether multiple simultaneous MedDetectives investigations can operate without collapsing immersion, pacing, or collaborative reasoning.

The goal is NOT simply concurrency.

The goal is preserving:
- emotional realism,
- differentiated escalation,
- facilitator attention balance,
- and room-first investigation integrity.

---

# Test Configuration

Recommended stress-test configuration:

- 2–4 simultaneous teams
- staggered escalation pacing
- independent contradiction release timing
- overlapping facilitator demands
- environmental transitions occurring simultaneously

---

# Critical Verification Areas

## Team Differentiation

Verify:
- teams maintain distinct reasoning pathways,
- escalation sequences diverge naturally,
- and collaborative interpretations remain independent.

Teams should not collapse into identical investigative experiences.

---

## Facilitator Attention Balance

Verify facilitators can:
- monitor multiple reasoning states,
- preserve immersion across teams,
- coordinate contradiction pacing,
- and avoid over-focusing on a single group.

---

## Escalation Stability

Verify:
- contradiction releases remain intentional,
- environmental pacing remains synchronized appropriately,
- and escalation intensity does not overwhelm facilitators.

---

# Drift Detection

Watch carefully for:
- facilitators becoming instructional,
- over-standardized participant experiences,
- escalation shortcuts,
- reduced ambiguity,
- or puzzle-race behavior between teams.

If these emerge, recalibration is required.

---

# Final Verification Standard

Successful multi-team orchestration feels:
- immersive,
- differentiated,
- emotionally grounded,
- collaboratively unstable,
- and narratively alive.

Participants should feel they experienced a living investigation rather than a synchronized classroom activity.
